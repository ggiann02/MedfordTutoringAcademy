About the ACT

The #1 tutoring academy in Medford, MA

The ACT is a college entrance exam that most colleges and universities use to determine admissions. The goal of the ACT is to assess a high school student's college preparation and give universities with a single point of comparison for all candidates.

The ACT is taken by the majority of high school students in the spring of their junior year or the fall of their senior year. It's critical to give yourself enough time to retake the test if you need to improve your score before applying to college.

There are four ACT sections: English, Reading, Math, and Science.

The ACT is 2 hours and 55 minutes long. If you choose to take the ACT with Essay, the test will be 3 hours and 35 minutes long.

The ACT is graded on a scale of 1 to 36 for each section. The average of your four section scores is your composite ACT score, which is also on a scale of 1 to 36. If you decide to take the ACT with Writing Test, you will receive a separate score on the Writing Test.

Please visit the ACT registration website for more details.
